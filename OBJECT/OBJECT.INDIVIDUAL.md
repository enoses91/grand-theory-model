## 1. Base Definition (Oâ°)

**An Individual is the smallest analytic unit that carries cognitive capacity, traits, incentive exposure, and experience.**

Individuals answer:

> *How do constraints and incentives register at the micro level?*

They do **not** answer:
- who governs,
- who coordinates,
- who acts at scale,
- why regimes change.

Individuals are **carriers**, not controllers.

## 3. Selection vs Treatment Discipline (Locked)

### 3.1 Selection (dominant)

Most observed population outcomes reflect **selection**, not intervention:

- educational sorting
- occupational sorting
- group membership
- geographic mobility
- survival and persistence under stress

Selection operates through **constraints**, not intent.

## 6. Incentive Exposure (Projection, Not Choice)

Individuals experience local incentive gradients generated by:

- metric variables (`COR.PHI`, `COR.THETA`, `COR.E`, `COR.X`),
- state variables (`COR.KAPPA`, `COR.H`),
- active fields (Market, Information, Governance, Violence, etc.),
- class position and exit asymmetry.

Incentives are **emergent projections** of system structure, not preferences.

## 7. Exit, Compliance, and Exposure

At the individual level:

- **Exit pressure** depends on \(X_i^{eff}\) and class.
- **Compliance pressure** depends on COR.PHI, enforcement asymmetry, and `COR.H`.
- **Variance exposure** depends on class and regime configuration.

Observed behavior reflects **constraint response**, not moral choice.
